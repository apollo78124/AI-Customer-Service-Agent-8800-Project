
## **Forensic Investigation (Crime and Intelligence Analysis Option), Advanced Certificate, Part-time (528AADCERT) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Advanced Certificate in Crime & Intelligence Analysis**  
- **Delivery:** **Blended (Online & On-Campus, Part-time)**  
- **Objective:**  
  - Trains professionals in **crime and intelligence analysis for law enforcement and regulatory bodies**  
  - Develops **tactical, operational, and strategic intelligence products**  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Crime Mapping & Data Analytics**  
- **National Security & Major Crimes Analysis**  
- **Organized Crime & Open-Source Intelligence**  
- **Cybercrime & Social Media Investigations**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intakes:** **January, April, September**  
- **Completion timeline:** **Up to 7 years (Flexible Learning)**  
- **Class Schedule:** **Evenings & Weekends**  

### **Core Courses**
- **FSCT 8431 - Applied Research Methods & Statistics in Crime Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8432 - Reasoning for Crime and Intelligence Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8434 - Crime Mapping (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8436 - Introduction to Crime and Intelligence Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8430 - Organized Crime Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8481 - Major Crimes Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8482 - National Security Analysis (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8486 - Strategic Analysis (3.0 credits)**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **Minimum of 60 post-secondary credits in Social Sciences or related field**  
- **English 12 (67%) or equivalent**  
- **Criminal Record Check (CRC) required**  

### **Career Paths**
- **Crime Analyst**  
- **Intelligence Analyst**  
- **National Security Analyst**  
- **Open-Source Investigator**  
- **Strategic Intelligence Analyst**

## **Forensic Investigation (Digital Forensics and Cybersecurity Option), Bachelor of Technology, Full-time/Part-time (847CBTECH) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Bachelor of Technology in Digital Forensics & Cybersecurity**  
- **Delivery:** **Full-time (Downtown Campus) & Part-time (Online & Blended)**  
- **Objective:**  
  - Prepares students for **cybercrime investigations, digital forensics, and IT security roles**  
  - Teaches **expert witness testimony, cybersecurity threats, and digital evidence recovery**  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Cybersecurity Threats & Incident Response**  
- **Digital Forensics & Evidence Analysis**  
- **Network Security & Vulnerability Assessments**  
- **Mobile & Cloud Forensics**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intakes:** **January & September (Full-time), January, April, September (Part-time)**  
- **Completion timeline:** **2 years (Full-time) or up to 7 years (Part-time)**  
- **Total Credits:** **62.0**  

### **Core Courses**
- **FSCT 7509 - Introduction to Digital Forensics & Evidence Imaging (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 7511 - Cybersecurity Foundations (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 7512 - Digital Forensics in the Cloud (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8500 - Mobile Forensics (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8513 - Digital Forensics 1 (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8560 - Network Security 2 (3.0 credits)**  
- **FSCT 8590 - Network Exploits & Vulnerabilities (3.0 credits)**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **Diploma or Bachelor's degree in Computing, IT, or Cybersecurity**  
- **Criminal Record Check (CRC) required**  
- **International Students:** **Eligible with a study permit (Full-time option only)**  

### **Career Paths**
- **Cybersecurity Analyst**  
- **Digital Forensic Examiner**  
- **Cybercrime Investigator**  
- **Network Security Specialist**

- **Delivery:** **Fully Online (Asynchronous, Part-time)**

  - Develops **basic cybersecurity investigation skills**  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Digital Evidence Handling & Imaging**  
- **Basic Mobile Forensics & Data Recovery**  
- **Cyber Threats & IT Security Fundamentals**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intakes:** **January, April, September**  
- **Completion timeline:** **Self-paced, 3-course sequence**  

### **Core Courses**
- **XFSC 3000 - Introduction to Digital Forensics (1.0 credit)**  
- **XFSC 3005 - Forensic Evidence Imaging (1.0 credit)**  
- **XFSC 3010 - Mobile Forensics Basic Skills (1.0 credit)**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **Recommended:** Post-secondary education in Computer Science or IT  
- **International Students:** **Eligible if studying from outside Canada (not for study permit holders)**

- **Cybersecurity Assistant**  
- **Digital Evidence Technician**  
- **IT Security Support**
