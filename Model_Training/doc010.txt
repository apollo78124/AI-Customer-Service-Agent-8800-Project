
## **Applied Computing, Master of Science, Full-time (M600MSC) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Graduate-Level Applied Computing Specialization**.
- **Delivery:** **In-person, Burnaby Campus**.
- **Objective:**  
  - Develop **technical leadership & research skills**.
  - Apply **computational methods across industries**.
  - Specialize in **AI, Machine Learning, Cybersecurity, Data Science**.

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Algorithm Analysis & Optimization**
- **Cybersecurity & Ethical AI**
- **Human-Centered Computing & UX**
- **Data Science & AI Model Development**
- **Applied Research in Computing**

### **Program Structure**
- **Duration:** **2 years full-time (4 terms, 15 weeks each)**.
- **Cohort-based model:** Students progress together for industry networking.
- **Intake:** **September**.

#### **Year 1: Core Courses**
- **Algorithm Analysis & Optimization**
- **Human-Centered Computing**
- **Applied AI & Machine Learning**
- **Cybersecurity for Applied Computing**
- **Applied Research Methods**

#### **Year 2: Specialization Paths**
- **Thesis Path:** Research-driven approach, recommended for PhD aspirants.
- **Project & Internship Path:** Industry placement + applied research project.

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **Standard Entry:**  
  - **Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science or Applied Computing (min. GPA 2.8/4.0)**.
- **Alternate Entry:**  
  - **Bachelor’s degree in another field (min. GPA 3.0/4.0) + computing experience**.
  - **Bridging courses may be required**.
- **International Students:**  
  - **Study permit required**.
  - **Internship option requires a co-op work permit**.
