

## **Climate Changemakers Leadership Training, Microcredential, Part-time (0861CM) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Sustainability & Climate Leadership Microcredential**.  
- **Delivery:** **Fully Online (Part-time)**.  
- **Objective:**  
  - Equip professionals with knowledge in **climate action & sustainability**.  
  - Help workers transition into **sustainability-focused careers**.  
  - Prepare industries with **climate-literate employees**.  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Earth & Climate Systems**  
- **Climate Action & Systems Transformation**  
- **Community Leadership in Climate Action**  
- **Workforce Readiness for Climate Jobs**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intakes:** **January, April, September**.  
- **Completion timeline:** **4-6 months (Flexible Learning)**.  
- **Schedule:** **Self-paced online courses**.  

### **Core Courses**
- **XCCL 1000 - Earth and Climate Systems (1.0 credit)**  
- **XCCL 1010 - Climate Action and Systems Transformation (1.0 credit)**  
- **XCCL 1020 - Community Climate Leadership (1.0 credit)**  
- **XCCL 1030 - Climate Workforce Readiness (1.0 credit)**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **No formal application required** (email program contact for credential upon completion).  
- **International Students:** **Eligible if studying from outside Canada (not for study permit holders).**  

### **Career Paths**
- **Sustainability Coordinator**  
- **Climate Action Consultant**  
- **Environmental Policy Advisor**  
- **Corporate Sustainability Strategist**

## **Combined Honours in Biochemistry and Forensic Science, Bachelor of Science, Full-time (9940BSC) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Joint UBC-BCIT Interdisciplinary BSc in Biochemistry & Forensic Science**.  
- **Delivery:** **In-person (UBC & BCIT Campuses)**.  
- **Objective:**  
  - Provide advanced training in **biochemistry, forensic science, and molecular biology**.  
  - Prepare students for **careers in forensic science, biomedical research, and biotechnology**.  
  - Offer **a pathway to graduate school, medical school, or law school**.  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Forensic Biology & DNA Typing**  
- **Molecular Biochemistry & Genetics**  
- **Analytical Chemistry & Instrumental Analysis**  
- **Quality Assurance in Forensic Science**  
- **Criminal Law & Expert Witness Testimony**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intake:** **September**.  
- **Duration:** **4 years (UBC Year 1, BCIT + UBC Years 2-4)**.  
- **Co-op Option:** **Optional paid work terms (4-16 months).**  

### **Core Courses**
#### **Year 1 (UBC)**
- **CHEM 121 & 123 - Principles of Chemistry**  
- **BIOL 112 - Cell Biology**  
- **MATH 100 - Differential Calculus**  
- **ENGL 112 - University Writing**  

#### **Year 2 (BCIT & UBC)**
- **FSCT 7320 - Introduction to Forensic Science**  
- **BIOC 203 - Fundamentals of Biochemistry**  
- **BIOL 234 - Genetics**  
- **CHEM 203 - Organic Chemistry**  

#### **Year 3 (BCIT & UBC)**
- **FSCT 7910 - Research Methodology for Forensic Science**  
- **FSCT 8150 - DNA Typing & Evidence Recovery**  
- **BIOC 301 - Biochemistry Lab**  
- **BIOL 335 - Molecular Genetics**  

#### **Year 4 (BCIT & UBC)**
- **FSCT 7009 - Law for Forensic Science**  
- **FSCT 8160 - DNA Typing Applications**  
- **BIOC 402 - Protein Structure & Function**  
- **BIOC 410 - Nucleic Acid Structure & Function**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **Apply via UBC Admissions (competitive entry).**  
- **Minimum GPA: 70% (Honours-level entry requirements).**  
- **Criminal Record Check required for forensic coursework.**  
- **International Students:** **Eligible with a study permit.**  

### **Career Paths**
- **Forensic Scientist (Crime Labs, RCMP, FBI, Private Sector)**  
- **Molecular Biochemist (Biotech, Pharma, Research Labs)**  
- **Medical Researcher (Cancer Research, Genetics, Virology)**  
- **Pathway to Medical School, Law School, or Grad School**

## **Computer Information Systems Administration, Diploma, Full-time (1930DIPMA) - BCIT**
### **Program Overview**
- **Type:** **Full-time Diploma in IT Systems & Network Administration**.  
- **Delivery:** **In-person (Burnaby Campus)**.  
- **Objective:**  
  - Train students in **network infrastructure, cybersecurity, and IT system management**.  
  - Provide hands-on experience with **Cisco, Microsoft, Linux, and cloud technologies**.  

### **Key Learning Areas**
- **Network Configuration & Troubleshooting**  
- **Windows/Linux Server Administration**  
- **Cloud Computing & Virtualization (AWS, VMware, Azure)**  
- **Enterprise Security & Cyber Threat Management**  

### **Program Structure**
- **Intake:** **September**.  
- **Duration:** **2 years (4 levels, 140 credits total).**  
- **Schedule:** **Monday-Friday, 8:30 AM - 5:30 PM**.  

### **Core Courses**
#### **Year 1**
- **CISA 1310 - Network Essentials**  
- **CISA 1320 - Desktop Support & Maintenance**  
- **CISA 1350 - Database Essentials**  
- **CISA 1360 - Programming for IT Administrators**  

#### **Year 2**
- **CISA 2310 - IP Routing & Switching**  
- **CISA 2370 - Network Security Fundamentals**  
- **CISA 3330 - Deploying Windows Services**  
- **CISA 3370 - Enterprise Network Security**  

### **Admissions & Requirements**
- **English 12 (67%) & Math 11 (60%) or equivalent**.  
- **International Students:** **Eligible with a study permit**.  

### **Career Paths**
- **Network Administrator (Enterprise IT, Government, Healthcare, Finance)**  
- **IT Security Analyst (Cybersecurity, Cloud Security, DevSecOps)**  
- **Systems Engineer (Cloud Infrastructure, Data Centers, Virtualization)**  
- **Opportunities for Certifications (Cisco CCNA, Microsoft Azure, AWS, Linux RHCSA)**
